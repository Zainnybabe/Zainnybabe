Alhaji Chief (Dr.) Sikiru Ololade Ayinde Balogun

Sikiru Ayinde (9 February 1948 - 16 December 2010) better known by his stage name, Ayinde Barrister was a Nigerian-born Yoruba singer-songwriter, songproducer and music performer.
He is regarded as a pioneer of Fuji and Wéré music.
After his first break into music in 1965, Ayinde Barrister went on to release over 70 studio albums.

Here's a timeline of Sikiru Ololade Ayinde Balogun

  • 1948 - Born in Lagos, Lagos State, Nigeria, to the family of Salawu Balogun who was a trader from Ibadan,and his father was a butcher.
  • 1966 - Ayinde Barrister released his first LP record
  • 1977 - He released Bismillahi
  • 1982 - He had a bitter feud with another Fuji singer, Kollington Ayinla
  • 1983 - he was awarded an honorary PhD in music at the City University of Los Angeles
  • 1984 - he bagged two chieftaincy titles in two historic cities - the Otunbaala of Lagos and Agbaakin Bobagunwa of Ibadan.
  • 1986 - he was presented with the honorary citizenship and Key to the City of Providence, USA.
  • 1988 - he was honored by the Kano Emirate as the Sarkin Waka (King of Music).
  • 1989 - he was honoured as the Musical Legend of the Commonwealth, during his European Summer Tour, By Britain-based Nigerian National Union.
  • 2004 - He had a popular record titled Reality
  • 2006 - he was honored with the title of Member of the Order of the Federal Republic (MFR).
